Open Positions ‚Äì Fall 2025

The Center for Applied Artificial Intelligence (CAAI) is now accepting new members and collaborators for the upcoming academic term. We have openings for graduate research positions (2) and undergraduate research internships (2). Graduate research positions are open to students interested in AI, computer vision, machine learning, or cross-modal applications, and candidates may join either as students of The Korean Academy (such as TOPIK holders preparing for Korean university admission) or by demonstrating strong skills in programming (e.g., Python, machine learning frameworks, data science) and research. These positions are ideal for highly motivated individuals aiming to build a strong research profile for future graduate studies or international PhD programs, and for those willing to contribute to paper writing, model development, and experiments. Lab members will have access to resources such as dedicated workspace and equipment, and will receive guidance on research publishing and opportunities to co-author international conference or journal papers.

Undergraduate research internships are available for 3rd or 4th year undergraduates passionate about AI and seeking hands-on research experience. Interns will contribute to ongoing projects through programming, testing, dataset handling, and literature review, and will benefit from mentorship by graduate-level researchers, involvement in real research workflows, and recommendation or experience certificates for academic or industry applications. These internships are ideal for students planning to pursue higher education (MS/PhD) or to enter the IT industry with strong research and project experience.

If you are interested in joining, please send an email with your CV, academic transcripts (undergraduate and/or graduate, if available), and a short research statement or project portfolio to gurpreetsinghmcse@gmail.com. For questions or informal inquiries, feel free to reach out anytime.

About CAAI Lab

The CAAI Lab (Center for Applied Artificial Intelligence) is an experimental eLAB focused on supporting students who are new to academic research and wish to enter the field of artificial intelligence, machine learning, computer vision, and cross-modal systems. The lab is designed as a guided research environment where beginners can learn research methodologies, implementation practices, paper writing, and experimentation under structured mentorship.

CAAI Lab operates under The Korean Academy, an MSME-registered organization in India. Through this association, the lab plays an important academic support role for students learning the Korean language and preparing for future academic or professional transitions to Korea or other countries. The lab environment is especially beneficial for students aiming to build a research profile while preparing for international higher education opportunities.

CAAI Lab itself does charge any joining or participation fee. However, joining under The Korean Academy involving TOPIK Level 5 or Level 6 holders who are enrolled as students under The Korean Academy can join the lab with no charge, applicable academy-level policies may apply. Students who are new to research and wish to learn more about joining criteria or fee-related matters should refer to the official website of The Korean Academy or contact admission@thekoreancad.com for accurate and updated information.

Research projects at CAAI Lab typically span a duration of approximately three (3) months. Upon successful completion of the assigned research objectives and submission of a research paper or equivalent deliverable, eligible participants may receive a Certificate of Research Participation and, where appropriate, a Letter of Recommendation based on performance, contribution, and commitment.
