Research Projects

[1] Analyzing Gender Bias in Media Using AI: A Cross-Cultural Study of Representation, Language, and Imagery (2025.7.21 - 2025.9.30): This flagship project investigates gender bias in media content across cultures by leveraging natural language processing, computer vision, and ethical frameworks. The study aims to uncover patterns of representation and language, and to develop AI tools that promote fairness and inclusivity in media analysis.

[2] Business Growth and Management Research Team Formation (2023 - 2024, finished): In 2023, a research team was formed to explore the intersection of business growth, management, and AI. Members Simran Singh, Nahid Rehmani, Pushpa Kumari, and Vedha Varshini worked on projects that applied AI-driven analytics to optimize business strategies and foster innovation across industries. The project was successfully completed in 2024.

[3] Cybersecurity and AI: Assessing Vulnerabilities in Modern Infrastructure (Spring 2024): Gurpreet Singh and Prof. Saurabh Singh conducted an in-depth analysis of the vulnerabilities and impacts of open SSH ports on CENTOS 9 virtual machines hosted on Mac ARM computers (CVE-2023-25136). The research provides practical recommendations for securing cloud and virtualized environments in the age of AI-driven cyber threats.

[4] ChatGPT and Cybersecurity: Implications and Repercussions (2022): In collaboration with Prof. Preethi Ananthachari, Gurpreet Singh explored the impact of large language models like ChatGPT on cybersecurity. The project, published in a leading journal, discusses both the opportunities and risks posed by generative AI in the context of digital security and privacy.
